The correlation between numerical features:

Which attribute is most correlated with heart disease?


Heart disease is correlated with most features.
ST slope, maximum heart rate and chest pain types that could be clinical indications of heart disease are not correlated with heart disease.
Fasting blood sugar is slightly negative correlated with heart disease.
The strongest unmodifiable risk factors are age and sex; the strongest modifiable risk factors are exercise angina and ST depression.
Blood pressure, cholesterol levels and fasting blood sugar do not have strong collinearity with each other.
No strong correlated features in the dataset.
